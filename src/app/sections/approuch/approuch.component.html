Beadwork Industry

Ushanga Kenya initiative plays the role of cultural preservation of beadwork for socio-economic empowerment, capacity building and value addition through training on new beading techniques, use of modern and efficient building tools and new contemporary designs competitive both in local and international markets .

Jewelery

Jewellery has been one of the most powerful ornaments amongst the pastoralist women as it expresses their beauty, tradition and their resilience. Ornaments such as neckpieces, rings and bracelets are beaded in different colors which express their character, environment and symbolism for example the creation of the Johari bracelet which symbolises standing against Female Genital Mutilation, in addition men also adorned themselves in different types of jewellery to show their status within the community. These pieces have have been added some modern touch to create contemporary modern pieces.

Home Furnishings

Products used for decorating the home. Incorporating traditional beadwork into contemporary everyday home furnishings has been acceptable globally. Most of the decorative pieces in homes are a show piece and our women have worked tirelessly to ensure that their traditional pieces become a statement piece in every home. They include beaded mirrors beaded pillows and coasters and table mats. incorporating beads into home furnishings is a unique way to add some personality and style to your living space. Beads can be used in a variety of ways such as on curtains and lampshades to create stunning patterns and effects as well as on cushions or throw pillows to add texture and interest. Additionally beads can be incorporated into decorative pieces such as vases , flower pots and even picture frames or mirrors to add a touch of glamour and sophistication .Not only do beads add visual appeal they also provide a tactile experience which can enhance the overall ambiance odf a room , it is also an excellent way to showcase your creativity and give your home a un ique and personal touch .
